Crown Shyness

Crown shyness (also canopy disengagement,[1] canopy shyness,[2] or inter-crown spacing [3]) is a phenomenon observed in some tree species, in which the crowns of fully stocked trees do not touch each other, forming a canopy with channel-like gaps.[4][5] The phenomenon is most prevalent among trees of the same species, but also occurs between trees of different species.[6][7]There exist many hypotheses as to why ÃŸcrown shyness is an adaptive behavior, and research suggests that it might inhibit spread of leaf-eating insect arvae.[8]
